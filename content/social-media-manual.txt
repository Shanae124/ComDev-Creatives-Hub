1. Introduction to Social Media 2025
MODULE ONE | FOUNDATION
Social media has transformed from a novelty communication tool into the backbone of global digital
commerce, news distribution, and community building. In 2025, over 5.24 billion people use social media —
representing approximately 64% of the world's population. The average person maintains accounts on more
than seven platforms and spends over 2 hours and 20 minutes per day actively engaging with social content.
For businesses, this reality creates both extraordinary opportunity and intense competition. Organic reach
has declined on legacy platforms like Facebook, yet new platforms and formats — particularly short-form
video — have opened entirely new channels where brands can achieve viral growth with zero ad spend.
Understanding the current landscape is the essential first step.

PLATFORM SELECTION STRATEGYThe biggest mistake new businesses make is trying to be everywhere at once. A focused presence on two or
three well-chosen platforms consistently outperforms a scattered approach across every network. Use these
questions to guide your platform selection:
■ Where does your target audience spend the most time? (Research their demographics.)
■ What type of content can you consistently produce? (Video, photos, articles, audio?)
■ What is your primary business goal? (Brand awareness, leads, sales, community?)
■ What are your competitors doing — and where are they succeeding?
■ What human and technical resources do you have available?
"The best social media platform is the one where YOUR audience lives and
where YOU can consistently show up with great content."

2. Corporate Groundwork & Brand Identity
MODULE ONE | FOUNDATION
Great marketing begins before you write a single post. The foundational elements of your brand — your
name, logo, colours, tone of voice, and value proposition — form the bedrock upon which every piece of
social content is built. In 2025, consumers form a judgment about a brand in less than 0.05 seconds online.
Getting these fundamentals right is not optional; it is the difference between a brand people remember and
one they scroll past.
BUSINESS NAME ESSENTIALSA business name is your first brand impression. It must work across your website, social handles, email
addresses, and verbal word-of-mouth simultaneously.
Key Checklist for a Strong Business Name:
■ Short & memorable — ideally 1–2 words, under 15 characters
■ Easy to pronounce and spell a single way
■ Available as a .com domain and across major social platforms
■ No unintended meanings or negative connotations in other languages
■ Not trademarked by another company in your industry
■ Future-proof — does not tie you to a single product, location, or trend
Quick Tool: Check Name Availability
Namecheckr.com — checks username availability across 30+ social platforms at once.
Godaddy.com or Namecheap.com — check and register domain names.
USPTO.gov / local IP authority — search for trademark conflicts.

3. Logo, Colour & Visual Identity Design
MODULE ONE | FOUNDATION
Your visual identity is the most instantly recognisable part of your brand. In the social media feed, thumbnails
and profile pictures appear at very small sizes — often just 40x40 pixels. A great logo works at any size and
remains identifiable even without colour.
LOGO DESIGN PRINCIPLES (2025)Logo design principles have not fundamentally changed, but the contexts in which logos appear have
multiplied dramatically. Your logo must now work as a tiny app icon, a large billboard, an animated GIF intro,
a profile picture, a watermark on video, and an embroidered patch — sometimes all in the same week.
The Five Principles of an Effective Logo:
■ Simplicity — complex logos fail at small sizes and are harder to remember
■ Versatility — must work in full colour, single colour, and on dark and light backgrounds
■ Memorability — a unique visual hook that sticks in the mind after a single viewing
■ Timelessness — avoid trendy fonts or effects that date quickly (e.g. 'gradient overload')
■ Relevance — subtly communicates the industry, values, or personality of the brand

4. Facebook for Business (2025)
MODULE TWO | FACEBOOK & META
With 3.07 billion monthly active users, Facebook remains the world's most-used social platform in 2025.
While younger audiences gravitate toward TikTok and Instagram, Facebook dominates among users aged
25–54 and is the #1 platform in most developing markets. For businesses targeting these demographics,
Facebook is non-negotiable.
FACEBOOK ACCOUNT TYPESPersonal Profile
A personal profile is a non-commercial account for individual use. Facebook's terms of service prohibit using
personal profiles for business purposes. Always create a Business Page for your company. However, your
personal profile is used to create and administer your Page.
Facebook Business Page
A Business Page is a public-facing account for brands, businesses, celebrities, and public figures. Key 2025
features include: AI-powered Post Composer suggestions, integrated Facebook Shop, Appointment Booking,
automatic WhatsApp link integration, and advanced Meta Business Suite analytics.
Facebook Groups
Groups are community spaces where members share content and interact. In 2025, groups represent
Facebook's highest organic reach channel — group posts routinely reach 40–60% of members compared to
less than 5% for regular Page posts. Building a branded community group alongside your Page is a powerful
growth strategy.
SETTING UP A FACEBOOK BUSINESS PAGE (2025)Step 1: Log into your personal Facebook account at facebook.com
Step 2: Click the menu (grid icon) → Select 'Pages' → Click 'Create New Page'
Step 3: Enter your Page Name (use your exact business name), Category, and Bio (up to 255 characters)
Step 4: Upload your Profile Picture (logo, minimum 180x180px — appears circular)
Step 5: Upload your Cover Photo or Video (820x312px for desktop, 640x360px for mobile)
Step 6: Complete your 'About' section: website, phone, address, hours, and services
Step 7: Set up your Call-to-Action button (options: Book Now, Shop Now, Contact Us, etc.)
Step 8: Enable WhatsApp Chat link if applicable
Step 9: Begin posting content before inviting followers (aim for at least 3–5 posts)
Step 10: Invite your existing contacts to Like/Follow the page

THE FACEBOOK ALGORITHM IN 2025Facebook's algorithm — now called EdgeRank's successor, the Meta Content Recommendation System —
prioritises content based on four key factors:
■ Relationship: Content from Pages/people you interact with regularly ranks higher
■ Content Type Preference: The algorithm learns and serves formats you engage with most
■ Meaningful Interactions: Comments and shares are weighted 5–10x more than Likes
■ Recency: Newer content is favoured, but evergreen viral content gets extended reach
Algorithm Hack: The 'Comments First' Strategy
Instead of asking followers to 'Like' your post, ask a question that invites a comment.
Comments generate 5-10x more algorithm points than Likes, significantly boosting organic reach.
Example: Instead of 'Like if you agree!', try 'What's your biggest challenge with [topic]? Comment below!'

FACEBOOK REELS IN 2025Facebook Reels — introduced in 2022 and rapidly grown since — now receive significantly higher organic
reach than any other content type on Facebook. Meta has publicly stated its commitment to prioritizing video
content, particularly Reels, across both Facebook and Instagram in its algorithm.
Reels Best Practices:
■ Length: 7–30 seconds performs best for reach; 30–90 seconds for storytelling
■ Hook: First 1–3 seconds must be visually arresting — no slow introductions
■ Captions: 85% of videos are watched without sound — always add captions
■ Music: Use trending audio from the Facebook Reels audio library
■ Text Overlays: Key messages should appear on screen as text
■ Call to Action: End every Reel with a clear action ('Follow for more', 'Link in bio')
■ Posting Frequency: 3–5 Reels per week outperforms sporadic posting

THE CONTENT MIX: 80/20 RULEThe most effective Facebook pages follow the 80/20 content rule: 80% of content provides value,
entertainment, or education — with no direct sales message. Only 20% of content directly promotes products
or services. Pages that reverse this ratio see rapid follower decline and suppressed algorithmic reach.

AUDIENCE RESEARCH IN 2025Understanding your target audience has never been easier — or more important. Modern tools allow you to
build rich audience personas based on real behavioral data, not assumptions.
Audience Research Tools:
■ Meta Audience Insights (business.facebook.com) — demographic data for any interest group
■ SparkToro — discover where your audience spends time online, what podcasts they listen to
■ Google Analytics 4 — demographic and interest data for your website visitors

THE ANATOMY OF A VIRAL SHORT-FORM VIDEOAfter analysing thousands of viral short-form videos, a consistent structure emerges that maximises both
algorithm performance and viewer completion rates:
■ 0–1 seconds: The Hook — a visually striking image, bold text, or spoken statement that stops the scroll
■ 1–3 seconds: The Promise — explicitly state what the viewer will get ('Here's how I grew to 10k in 30 days')
■ 3–15 seconds: The Value — deliver the core content quickly, using text overlays and voiceover
■ 15–25 seconds: The Proof — evidence, example, result, or demonstration
■ 25–30 seconds: The CTA — clear instruction ('Follow for more', 'Comment YES if this helped')
2025 Video Hook Formulas That Work

4. 'POV: You just [relatable scenario]' — POV format drives massive engagement
5. 'Wait for it...' — teases payoff, drives full completion (critical for algorithm)

THE YOUTUBE ALGORITHM IN 2025YouTube's recommendation algorithm has one primary goal: keep viewers on YouTube longer. It achieves
this by recommending videos that have historically been watched to completion by similar viewers. This
means average view duration and viewer satisfaction (likes, comments, surveys) are the most powerful
signals.
Algorithm Optimisation Tips:
■ Open every video with a strong hook that delivers on the thumbnail/title promise
■ Use pattern interrupts every 60–90 seconds to maintain attention (cuts, graphics, music changes)
■ Use the 'Loop Method': reference something coming later in the video to prevent early drop-offs
■ End videos with a compelling reason to watch another video immediately
■ Upload consistently — the algorithm rewards channels that post on predictable schedules

PLATFORM COPYRIGHT SYSTEMSYouTube Content ID:
YouTube's Content ID system automatically scans every uploaded video against a database of copyrighted
works. A match results in a copyright claim (not a strike), which may mute your audio, block the video in
certain countries, or redirect ad revenue to the rights holder.
Meta Rights Manager:
Facebook and Instagram use Rights Manager to detect copyrighted images and video. Rights holders can
register their content and choose to block, monetize, or monitor infringing posts.
TikTok Copyright Policy:
TikTok's Commercial Music Library provides licensed music for business accounts. Using unlicensed
commercial music in business account videos risks removal or account restrictions.
